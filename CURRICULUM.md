# Curriculum

9 units. Each unit is 1-3 lessons. Total ~15 lessons to target level.
Order is by "how soon you'll need it at Haneda/on the course", not by textbook logic.

## Unit 0 — Sound system + kana + Korean cheat codes (3-4 lessons)

The 5 sounds Koreans get wrong, long vowels, double consonants, pitch of politeness.
The particle map (은/는→wa, 이/가→ga, 을/를→o...) and 20 instant cognates.
**Kana track (Henry's call, 2026-07-04): learn hiragana + katakana properly.**
Hangul glosses distort exactly the sounds that matter; kana is the real sound map
(1 kana = 1 beat). Katakana is priority for the golf/tourist domain — most target
vocab is katakana loanwords (ビール, カート, ロッカー, ナイスショット).
Handwritten practice required — this is the one unit where pen beats voice.
Kanji stays permanently out of scope.
After this: you can pronounce and READ (kana-level) anything in later lessons.

## Unit 1 — Survival core (2 lessons)

Greetings, thank you / sorry / excuse me, yes/no, this/that/which,
"~ kudasai" (주세요), "~ arimasu ka?" (있어요?), "doko desu ka?" (어디예요?),
"wakarimasen" (모르겠어요), "mou ichido" (다시 한번).
After this: you survive any counter interaction by pointing + these phrases.

## Unit 2 — Numbers, money, shopping (2 lessons)

1-10,000, prices (en), counters you actually need (개→ko, 명→mei/nin, 잔→hai),
"ikura desu ka?" (얼마예요?), card/cash, bags, tax-free, konbini flow.
After this: you buy anything without freezing at the register.

## Unit 3 — Restaurant and bar (2 lessons)

Getting a table (인원수), ordering ("~ o kudasai", "~ ni shimasu"), recommendations
("osusume wa?"), beer/highball/sake vocabulary, allergies/no-X requests,
check please ("okaikei onegaishimasu"), splitting.
After this: full restaurant cycle solo, izakaya included.

## Unit 4 — Getting around (1-2 lessons)

Taxi ("~ made onegaishimasu"), station basics, hotel check-in/out,
"how long does it take?" (dono kurai kakarimasu ka?), golf course shuttle pickup.
After this: airport → hotel → golf course without English roulette.

## Unit 5 — Golf course logistics (2 lessons)

Check-in (yoyaku shite imasu — 예약했어요), locker, rental clubs/shoes,
cart (kaato), caddie (kyadii-san), tee time, front nine / back nine (out/in),
lunch break culture (Japan golf = mandatory lunch turn), bath/onsen after round,
paying and precheck.
After this: the entire Japanese golf day flow, which is different from Korea's.

## Unit 6 — On-course talk (2 lessons)

"naisu shotto!", "naisu on", "naisu paa", club names in Japanese pronunciation,
score words (baadii, paa, bogii, daboh), honorary "OB desu ne...",
"saki ni douzo" (먼저 치세요), wind/distance talk ("nan yaado?").
After this: you sound like someone who plays golf, not a phrasebook.

## Unit 7 — Small talk with Japanese golfers (2 lessons)

Where are you from ("kankoku kara kimashita"), how often do you play,
handicap talk, "Japan courses are beautiful" compliments, weather,
inviting/being invited to drinks after, exchanging LINE, polite goodbyes.
After this: the target level — a pleasant 하프타임 lunch conversation.

## Unit 8 — Problem solving and repair (1 lesson)

Didn't catch it, speak slowly, "what does X mean?", lost item, wrong order,
feeling sick, calling front desk. The unit that makes all other units safe to fail in.

## Milestones

- After Unit 2: solo konbini and shop runs
- After Unit 4: solo travel day
- After Unit 5: solo golf trip logistics
- After Unit 7: **target reached** — conversational on the course
